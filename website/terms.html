Last updated 17 July 2026 · The short version. Manta UI is open source, your box is yours, and we don't warranty anything — but we'll do our best.

These terms cover the desktop app, the phone client (PWA today, native apps in flight), and the operated relay at relay.mantaui.com. They do not cover anything your AI agent does inside your tmux session — that's between you and your model provider.

Your box is yours

Manta UI is software you run on hardware you own. The server runs under your user account on your Linux box; the desktop app runs on your Mac; the phone client runs on your phone. You are responsible for what runs on those machines and what your agents do with that access.

We do not host your code, your sessions, your files, or your transcripts. Everything you produce lives in ~/.manta/ on your box.

The relay is our service

If you choose to reach your box through the operated relay (relay.mantaui.com), you are using a service we run. The relay routes traffic between your phone and your box, and stores the minimum needed to do that — see the Privacy page.

  • The relay accepts inbound HTTPS from your phone and proxies it to your box over the box's outbound WebSocket.
  • It does not inspect, log, or index the contents of your traffic.
  • It does enforce rate limits and a per-box byte meter (when subscription lands) — abuse will get a box rate-limited or dropped.

Acceptable use

Don't use Manta UI to:

  • Abuse the relay — no scanning, fuzzing, or volumetric traffic; we rate-limit per box.
  • Impersonate another box — the box↔relay handshake is trust-on-first-use; a box presenting a forged box_id loses its slot.
  • Reverse-engineer the relay to extract other users' traffic. (You can read the relay source — it's open source — but you cannot MITM another user's session.)
  • Use the relay as a general-purpose VPN, CDN, or proxy for non-Manta traffic.
  • Violate the law in any jurisdiction you operate from.
